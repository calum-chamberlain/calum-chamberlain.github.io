Student Projects

I do not currently have any funded PhD project opportunities, however we are always looking for more students interested in earthquake science using geophysical methods to join our research group. Interested students are encouraged to read the below statements about how to apply and contact Calum Chamberlain.

About VUW

Victoria University of Wellington—Te Herenga Waka is ranked in the top 100 institutions worldwide in Earth and Marine Sciences (2022 QS rankings). In the most recent Performance-Based Research Fund (PBRF) quality evaluation of New Zealand universities, the University was ranked first in New Zealand for overall research intensity and for research in Earth Sciences and Geography. The Geophysics programme is globally renowned for research in seismology, volcano and crustal geophysics, and Antarctic geophysics.

How to Apply

We invite applications for PhD and MSc projects from students with a relevent qualification at either MSc level for PhD application, or BSc level for MSc project. Students should hold these qualifications or will obtain this qualification before commencing their study in a relevant topic, which may include geophysics, earth sciences, geology or data science. International applications are extremely welcome, and New Zealand’s COVID-19 related border restrictions are beginning to ease. However we advise students to visit the Victoria University of Wellington site on international applications (wgtn.ac.nz/fgr/apply/international) and to familiarise themselves with New Zealand’s pandemic-related travel restrictions.

To apply, please first send an expression of interest by emailing a CV, academic transcript, and brief cover letter to Calum Chamberlain: calum.chamberlain@vuw.ac.nz.
